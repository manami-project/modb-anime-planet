    First year high schooler Junta Shiraishi is a mob character who goes unnoticed even when he's standing right next to you. But his classmate, "heroine-level beauty" Kubo, always notices him and is there to tease him. Anyone can become special to someone, but it might be a little too early to call these feelings "love." Perhaps this story is still two-steps from being a romantic comedy--let's call it a sweet comedy where a background character becomes visible!

    AustinDR Feb 14, 2023
    8

    This was really one of the cutest comfort shows of the year. It is a simple series about Kubo Nagisa's efforts of trying to make him stand out more.  At best, I thought the show's main gimmick would be this one guy is so unnoticeable, he could qualify as a NPC because virtually all the students and teachers do not notice him. Not even automatic sliding doors pick up on him. If that were to be the main idea for the show's entire run, it would get old really fast. But, the show works the way it does because of Kubo's interactions with Shiraishi where they bounce off each other. She is the one that playfully teases him providing the necessary progression the show needs. Really, the show is so cute, it often leaves me smiling like an idiot.
